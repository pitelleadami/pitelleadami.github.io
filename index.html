TO SIR WILLIAM DRAPER,
KNIGHT OF THE BATH.

March 3, 1769

Sir,

An academical education has given
you unlimited command over the
most beautiful figures of speech.
Masks, hatchets, racks and vipers,
dance through your letters in all
the mazes of metaphorical confusion.

These are the gloomy companions of
a disturbed imagination ;

the melancholy madness of poetry,


without the inspiration.

...

The Letters of Junius,
an excerpt from Letter VII
